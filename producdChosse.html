For nearly a century, many educators have conducted research and recorded the benefits of experiential learning, practice, and student-centered learning. Scientist Albert Einstein once said: “Example is not another way to teach, it is the only way to teach”. Almost every teacher understands the appeal of practical learning compared to purely theoretical classes.

Two authors Hoang Anh Duc and To Thuy Diem Quyen want to introduce a gentle and intuitive approach for both teachers and students to Project-Based Learning; using projects to make diverse, multi-dimensional examples that are as realistic as possible, thereby helping readers build their own knowledge and skills.

Project-Based Learning (PBL) is a model of organizing classes aimed at energizing traditional classes that focus on lessons and teachers. Project-Based Learning aims for long-term goals, centers on the learner, links content and integrates real-life practical issues. By stepping back, taking on the role of guide and empowering students to take control of their learning process, students form a sense of responsibility for their learning, thereby creating a more proactive learning environment.

The book consists of 4 main parts:

Part I: Introduction to Project-Based Learning
This part includes 3 chapters, introducing an overview of project-based learning, 6 learning characteristics of learning projects, and 8 steps to implement project-based learning in general education.

Part II: Implementing the Project
This part includes 8 chapters to help teachers and students know how to place a project in a suitable space, know how to implement a project from an existing space.

Part III: Introduction to Project-Based Learning
This part provides basic knowledge about different measures to evaluate Learning Projects, as well as different levels to evaluate learning activities.

Part IV: Appendix
This part expands some issues such as: STEM thinking in project-based learning, common problems and suggested solutions…. The book integrates with QR codes so that teachers and students can see illustrations of projects that have been implemented and won prizes in Creative Teacher competitions.
